MIT RCW 2024

MIT Robotics & Climate Workshop

May 7th, 2024 (8AM-4PM)

MIT Patil/Kiva Seminar Room (32-G449)

The MIT Robotics & Climate Workshop will be convening local members from the robotics, climate, and sustainability communities for a day of presentations, panels, and discussions about how robotics research can support climate change science, mitigation, and adaptation efforts.

Register Here!

Workshop Structure

The MIT Robotics & Climate Workshop will be a full-day event, with three distinct sessions. The day will begin with the theme of the built environment, with a keynote address by Prof. Christoph Reinhart and a subsequent panel discussion featuring Norhan Bayomi, Shide Salimi, and Ramon Weber. The second session will focus on climate science, with a keynote address by Prof. Abigail Bodner and a panel discussion featuring Lukas Lobert Taenzer, Bianca Champenois, and Alice Ren.

The day will conclude with a whitepaper brainstorming section, where participants will engage in teams to discuss future climate-focused robotics research. The ideas generated during this session will be used to inform the development of a white paper which will be a longer-term output of the workshop.

White Paper Details

Involvement in the white paper brainstorming session is not a requirement for participation in the workshop. Similarly, you can (and should) participate in the white paper session even if you do not desire to be involved in the future writing of the paper.

We anticipate the white paper will be a collaborative effort, which will take several months and will extend well beyond the local community of workshop participants. The white paper will attempt a comprehensive coverage of possible outlets for climate-relevant robotics research.

  • Christoph Reinhart is a building scientist and architectural educator working in the field of sustainable building design and environmental modeling. At MIT, he is the inaugural Alan and Terri Spoon Professor of Architecture and Climate, Director of the Building Technology Program and head of the Sustainable Design Lab (SDL), an inter-disciplinary group with a grounding in architecture that develops design workflows, planning tools and metrics to evaluate the environmental performance of buildings and neighborhoods. He is also a managing member at Solemma, a technology company and served as strategic development advisor for MIT spinoff mapdwell until it joined Palmetto Clean Technology in 2021. Planning tools originating from SDL and Solemma are used in practice and education in over 90 countries.
Abigail Bodner
Assistant Professor, MIT EAPS and EECS

  • Abigail Bodner is an Assistant Professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where she holds a shared position between the Departments of Earth, Atmospheric, and Planetary Sciences (EAPS), and Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS). Her research interests span climate, physical oceanography, geophysical fluid dynamics, and turbulence. She investigates turbulence in the upper ocean from O(100 km) to O(1 m) using a combination of theory, high-resolution numerical simulations, climate models, and machine learning. She also has a growing interest in coastal sea level drivers.
